# Designing for iPadOS

> iPad offers power, mobility, and flexibility for media, games, productivity, and creation.

**Platform:** iPadOS

## Device Characteristics

| | |
|---|---|
| **Display** | Large, high-resolution |
| **Ergonomics** | Held in hand or rested on surface/stand; viewing distance typically ≤3 ft |
| **Inputs** | Multi-Touch gestures, virtual/physical keyboard, pointing devices, Apple Pencil, Siri; often combines multiple input modes |
| **Usage pattern** | Quick actions to hours of immersion; multiple apps open simultaneously; split-screen and drag-and-drop between apps |

**Key system integrations:** Multitasking (Stage Manager, Split View, Slide Over), widgets, drag-and-drop.

## Best Practices

- Use the large display to elevate content — reduce modal interfaces and full-screen transitions. Position controls where they're accessible but out of the way.
- Use viewing distance and input mode to calibrate content size and density.
- Support Multi-Touch gestures, physical keyboard/trackpad, and Apple Pencil; consider unique interactions that combine multiple input modes.
- Adapt seamlessly to orientation, multitasking modes, Dark Mode, and Dynamic Type; support Mac Catalyst transition gracefully.
