# Design Principles

> Design principles guide tradeoffs and decisions across Apple platforms.

**Platforms:** iOS, iPadOS, macOS, tvOS, visionOS, watchOS

Use these principles as decision tools, not formulas. Apply them by understanding how people think, feel, and interact, then weighing competing priorities toward a useful, trustworthy, well-crafted experience.

## Principles

| Principle | Core directive | Apply by |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Make something meaningful. | Identify what matters most to the people using the product; focus on making those things great. |
| Agency | Let people do things their own way. | Help people act freely, stay informed, and recover from mistakes. |
| Responsibility | Act in people's best interest. | Prioritize safety and privacy; be transparent about what the product does and why. |
| Familiarity | Build on what people know. | Use established physical and digital patterns consistently. |
| Flexibility | Adapt to diverse contexts and needs. | Support different devices, interactions, perspectives, and accessibility needs. |
| Simplicity | Be clear and direct. | Remove unnecessary elements; make organization and navigation straightforward. |
| Craft | Care about every detail. | Deliver high-quality visuals, animation, wording, audio, reliability, and platform fit. |
| Delight | Make it human. | Shape the right emotional tone without obstructing the product's core purpose. |

## Purpose

- **Create value.** At every development stage, ask what the product is for and whether the design serves that purpose.
- **Keep focused.** Prioritize the most important features by aligning with how people want to use the product; make those features truly great.
- **Find new solutions.** Investigate existing solutions and avoid re-creating them. Define what sets the product apart and reflect that difference in the design.

## Agency

- **Stay out of the way.** Get people directly to the task or content. Keep the interface unobtrusive and present when needed.
- **Give freedom to explore.** Avoid locking people into specific flows or modes. If a guided flow is necessary, make it easy to skip or escape into the main experience.
- **Help recovery.** Build forgiveness into the design. Make reversing actions or returning to previous states easy so unexpected outcomes do not cost people time or work.

## Responsibility

- **Be transparent.** Make the app's intentions clear from the first interaction. Explain permission requests and disclose what data you collect and how you use it.
- **Protect information.** Collect only what the product needs to function. Handle data carefully, anticipate misuse or harm, and add protections against abuse and unintended consequences.

## Familiarity

- **Use known concepts.** Draw from real-world and software patterns people already understand.
- **Stay consistent.** Apply established visual and interaction behavior throughout the design so people can predict how new interactions work.
- **Provide clear feedback.** Signal control availability, content changes, alerts, and choices using system patterns.

## Flexibility

- **Design for everyone.** Treat accessibility as a priority from the start. Account for diverse experiences, perspectives, and needs.
- **Preserve context.** Keep content and controls in consistent, predictable positions as the design adapts across platforms and configurations; use natural animations to ease transitions.
- **Support varied input.** Design for as many inputs as possible, including voice, touch, keyboard, and other available methods.
- **Respect each platform.** Give every supported platform the same level of care; the product should feel polished and at home wherever it runs.

## Simplicity

- **Include only what is necessary.** Simplicity is not minimalism; keep important things close and let less-important things fall away.
- **Be concise.** Use exactly the words needed to convey a concept or label a control.
- **Establish hierarchy.** Make form and function apparent. Prioritize recognizable controls and consistent structure so people understand where they are and what comes next.

## Craft

- **Set the tone with quality.** Be deliberate about visuals, animation, wording, and audio.
- **Experiment and iterate.** Prototype early, discard what fails, refine each feature, and test in real-world settings for durability, reliability, and performance.
- **Maintain quality after shipping.** Keep the interface current with platform capabilities and design patterns.

## Delight

- **Choose the intended emotion.** Let the desired feeling shape the design: energizing, calming, thrilling, practical, whimsical, or another tone appropriate to the product.
- **Create defining moments.** Consider whether each interaction can express the product's character.
- **Do not use delight as decoration.** Do not let delight interfere with the core task. Balance practical refinement and whimsy based on the overall aesthetic.
- **Consider the whole experience.** Delight comes from the total effect of intent, focus, care, freedom to act, safety to explore, familiar metaphors, and flexible transitions.
