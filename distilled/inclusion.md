# Inclusion

> Inclusive apps put people first: respectful communication, accessible content, and experiences anyone can understand.

**Platforms:** iOS, iPadOS, macOS, tvOS, visionOS, watchOS

## Welcoming Language

- Use *you/your* to address people directly. Avoid *the user* or *the player* (feels distant).
- Reserve *we/our* for your software or company.
- Define all specialized or technical terms before using them.
- Replace colloquial expressions with plain language — many have exclusionary origins or are culture-specific (e.g., "peanut gallery," "grandfathered in").
- Avoid humor — subjective, hard to localize, risks alienating or insulting.
- Use clear, direct, respectful tone without unintended signals (e.g., academic tone implies education gatekeeping).

## Gender Identity

- Avoid unnecessary gender references. Prefer gender-neutral nouns and plural constructions over "his or her."
- Avoid gender-specific avatars, emoji, glyphs, or game characters for generic representations. Let people customize.
- For generic human figures, use nongendered images. SF Symbols provides nongendered `figure` and `person` symbols.
- If collecting gender (health/legal contexts): include *nonbinary*, *self-identify*, *decline to state*. Let people specify pronouns.

## People and Settings

- Represent a range of racial backgrounds, body types, ages, and physical capabilities in imagery.
- Avoid occupational stereotypes (male doctors, female nurses; heroes/villains mapped to demographics).
- Show relatable settings — avoid implying high affluence without reason.

## Avoiding Stereotypes and Assumptions

- Audit for hidden assumptions (e.g., "family" defaulting to mother/father/biological children).
- Security questions and similar inputs should reference universal human experiences, not culture/capability-specific ones. Bad: "What was your first car?" Good: "What's your favorite activity?"

## Accessibility

- Support VoiceOver, Display Accommodations, closed captioning, Switch Control, Speak Screen.
- Don't assume any disability prevents wanting to use your app — every experience should be accessible to everyone.
- Visual disabilities are a spectrum (low vision → blindness; includes color blindness, light sensitivity, peripheral loss).
- Everyone experiences temporary or situational disabilities (e.g., hearing loss from infection, noisy environment).
- Avoid imagery/copy that uses disability as a negative quality. Use people-first language.

## Languages and Localization

- Prepare for internationalization (locale-specific formatting, RTL layouts).
- Plain language, gender-neutral copy, and cultural neutrality ease localization.
- Use SF Symbols — provides language-specific glyphs and LTR/RTL variants.
- Research color meanings per locale — white means purity in some cultures, mourning in others.

## Platform Considerations

*No additional considerations for any platform.*
