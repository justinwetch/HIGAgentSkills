# Branding

> Express your unique brand in ways that feel at home on the platform and give people a consistent experience.

**Platforms:** iOS, iPadOS, macOS, tvOS, visionOS, watchOS

- Use your brand's unique **voice and tone** in all written copy.
- Consider specifying an **accent color** (applied to buttons, icons, interface elements). On macOS, users can override your accent with their system preference — respect this.
- Consider a **custom font** — ensure legibility at all sizes, including with Bold Text and larger accessibility type. Hybrid approach: custom font for headlines/subheadings, system font for body/captions (system fonts optimized for small-size legibility).
- **Content over branding** — don't use screen space for elements that only display a brand asset. Incorporate branding in refined, unobtrusive ways.
- **Use standard patterns consistently** — even highly stylized UI should place components in expected locations and use standard symbols for common actions.
- **Don't repeat your logo throughout the app** — people know which app they're in. Use the space for useful content and controls.
- **Don't use the launch screen as branding** — it disappears too quickly to convey information. Use a welcome or onboarding screen instead.
- **Follow Apple trademark guidelines** — Apple trademarks cannot appear in your app name or images.

## Platform Considerations

*No additional considerations for any platform.*
